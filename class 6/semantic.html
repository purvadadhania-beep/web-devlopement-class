facts about flowers

Flowers are the reproductive organs of flowering plants (angiosperms), which evolved about 140 million years ago to improve reproduction. They range from 0.1mm watermeal to 3-foot wide, foul-smelling Rafflesia arnoldii. Beyond beauty, they serve as food, medicine, and natural dyes.

The Largest Flower: The Titan Arum (Sumatran native) can grow up to 15 ft in height and 3 ft in width, while the Rafflesia arnoldii is the largest single flower.
