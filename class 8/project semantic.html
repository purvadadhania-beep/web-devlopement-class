Laptops are portable, all-in-one computers designed for mobility, typically weighing 2–5 lbs, featuring integrated screens, keyboards, and batteries. They revolutionized computing by enabling mobile work, with modern, sleek "ultrabooks" offering long battery life, while specialized models provide high-performance gaming or content creation capabilities.

Origins: The first laptop-sized computer was the Epson HX-20 (1981), while the Grid Compass (1982) pioneered the "clamshell" design used today.
